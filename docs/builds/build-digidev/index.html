The Goal üîó

Design a board using inexpensive 2-layer design.

The Challenge üîó

2-layer designs present challenges with RF compared to 4-layer designs, since the larger distance from signal layer to ground plane affects the RF negatively.

The Result üîó

Using the thinnest PCB stackup the board house offers (0.8mm), and adjusting the RF path accordingly (RF feedline 51–58–66 mil, keepaway 128 mil), the board has excellent RF performance while being a more cost efficient alternative to 4-layer designs.

The top layer contains components and signals, including 3.3V. The bottom layer is mostly GND as much as possible.

The antenna is a PCB monopole antenna, TI design as on the cc1310 Launchpad. I shortened the antenna to the single-band version, yet leaved a little for tuning purposes.

Power comes either from USB (which goes over a low-loss LDO to 3.3V) or coin cell battery. It can use either,

  • CR2477 (900–1000 mAh) but are hard to find in stores (eg not in Kjell, Clas Ohlson)
  • CR2450 (500–600 mAh), can be bought in most stores

These two are a bit wider in diameter than the most common CR2032 (24 vs 20 mm) and is thicker (5.0/7.7 vs 3.2 mm) but in turn have a much higher capacity (CR2032 typically 200-250 mAh) and the size increase isn’t too bad.

The battery input is polarity protected via a PMOS transistor

Programming is done over a Tag-connect connector.

First revision had a silly mistake where GND and power was switched in schematics on the RF module, but fixed to the second.
