Why ğŸ”—

We recently bought a soundbar system for our TV. The system includes a subwoofer. Not great, not terrible, but enough to potentially disturb neighbors. This calls for some kind of stand to separate the subwoofer-structure from the house-structure so the big badabooms doesn’t make for disturbance calls.

Also, the sides are dressed in a fabric mesh, and I want to raise it from the floor enough so that the vacuum cleaner won’t hit it.

What ğŸ”—

Surfing around, it seems two common approaches are either spikes, or rubber feet.

Spikes are used to ground a speaker to the floor through eg mats, while rubber feet are used to dampen vibrations transferring to the floor.

Hence, I built a heavy stand in concrete, with rubber feet.

Plate ğŸ”—

The plate is a thick slab of concrete. Rubble and stones were used to add strength, instead of rebar or wire mesh.

Base slab in concrete.

Four alu-wire things stick out, around which the feet are built.

Empty toilet rolls are used around the alu-wire, then concrete is added.

Concrete feet too.

After drying, the feet gets two layers of thick rubber, then one layer filt feet.

The concrete was old, so I guess it has absorbed some moisture. The strength was pretty poor, and parts and dust was coming off. I painted the whole thing in diluted PVA wood glue a few layers for strength, then added a plastic foil in almost-black, that would match the rest of the furniture.
