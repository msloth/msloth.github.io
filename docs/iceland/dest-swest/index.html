A fumarole is a vent in the surface from which hot volcanic gases and vapors are emitted, without any accompanying liquids or solids.

Fumaroles are characteristic of the late stages of volcanic activity, but fumarole activity can also precede a volcanic eruption and has been used for eruption prediction. Most fumaroles die down within a few days or weeks of the end of an eruption, but a few are persistent, lasting for decades or longer.

The predominant vapor emitted by fumaroles is steam. A fumarole that emits significant sulfur compounds is sometimes called a solfatara.
